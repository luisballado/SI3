Robotics is the science of perceiving and manipulating the physical world through computer-controlled mechanical devices

Robotics systems have in common that they are situaded in the physical wold, perceive their environments through sensors, and manipulate their environment through things that move.

As a result, robotics is moving into areas where sensor inputs becomes increasingly important, and where robot software has to be robust enough to cope with range of situations-- often too many to anticipate them all. Robotics, thus, is increasingly becoming a software science, where the goal is to develop robust software that enables robots to withstand the numerous challenges arising in unstructured and dynamics environments.

The key idea of probabilistic robotics is to represent uncertainty explicity, using the calculus of probability theory. Put differently, instead of relying on a single "best guess" as to what might be the case in world, probabilistic algorithms represent information by probability distributions over a whole space of possible hypotheses.

By basing control decisions on probabilistic information, these algorithms degrade nicely in the face of various sources of uncertainty.

Localization is the problem of estimating a robot's coordinates in an external reference frame from sensor data, using a map of the environment

In the probabilistic paradigm, the robot's momentary estimate (also called belief) is represented by a probability density function over the space of all locations.

In fact, certain probabilistic algorithms are currently the only know working solutions to hard robotic estimation problems

The robot is the ultimate mechatronic system.

The set of all configuration is called the configuration space.

An object is said to have n degrees of freedom (DOF) if its configuration can be minimally specified by n parameters. Thus, the number of DOF is equal to the dimension of the configuration space.

A rigid object in three-dimensional space has six DOF: three for positioning and three for orientation

The State Space - The state of a robot is a set of variables that, together with a description of the dynamics and future inputs, is sufficient to determine the future time response of the robot.
